Unbiasing Estimators

Theory

Being unbiased is generally considered a good property for an estimator to have, so it'd be nice if we could take a biased estimator and create an unbaised one. Fortunately, we often can.

Example 1: Maximum Likelihood of Uniform

As we have shown, the maximum likelihood estimator for $b$ in a continuous uniform distribution from 0 to $b$ is simply the maximum of our sample. We have also shown that $$E\left[m\right]=\frac{n}{n+1}b$$ In order for some estimator, $\hat{\theta}$ to be unbiased, it must be the case that $$E\left[\hat{\theta}\right]=\theta$$ Consider the estimator $\frac{n+1}{n}m$. We can show that this is unbaised: $$E\left[\frac{n+1}{n}m\right]=\frac{n+1}{n}E\left[m\right]=\frac{n+1}{n}\frac{n}{n+1}b=b$$ Presto! We have unbaised our estimator.
